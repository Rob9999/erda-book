# Mini Wording Set (canonical)

This set is an editorial reference to keep key terms consistent throughout the book.

## Weaponized Interdependence

**Core formula:** Dependence becomes leverage.

**Mnemonic:**

Interdependence is not a guarantee of peace when not all partners are allies.

**Standard sentence (short):**

Trade policy becomes security architecture once dependencies can be weaponised.

## Pragmatic Federalism

**Core formula:** Opt‑in capacity to act now, federal direction visible, democratically controlled.

**Standard sentence (short):**

Better capable with the willing than blocked with everyone – with an open door and anti‑sabotage safeguards.

## Unity-through-Action

**Core formula:** Unity emerges through joint action.

**Standard sentence (short):**

Not “unity first, action later”, but: action creates unity.

## Democratic Knowledge

**Core formula:** Democracy needs action-guiding knowledge about power, truth, security, technology and inner stance.

**Standard sentence (short):**

Democratic Knowledge connects truthfulness, power awareness and responsibility into democratic agency.

## Mosaic Principle

**Core formula:** Distributed capabilities become resilient when they are modular, redundant and democratically controlled.

**Standard sentence (short):**

The Mosaic Principle can be democratic only as a rule-of-law-controlled resilience architecture.

## Anti-Game-Over Principle

**Core formula:** Good systems avoid irreversible breakdowns and keep renewed development possible.

**Standard sentence (short):**

The Anti-Game-Over Principle protects learning capacity, dignity and the future against final failure.

## Prosperative Life

**Core formula:** Life flourishes when security, freedom, responsibility, joy and development work together.

**Standard sentence (short):**

Prosperative life is not merely prosperity, but open development in dignity, relation and responsibility.
