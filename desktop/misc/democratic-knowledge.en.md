# Democratic Knowledge

**Seven principles for democratic agency**

*ERDA Initiative – v2.5.0 · March 2026*

---

## 1) On power

Power is a tool, not a right. The stronger you are, the higher your burden of restraint and proof.

We do not reward terror, invasion, or blackmail — not out of sentiment, but because reward programmes repetition.

---

## 2) On dealing with non-democracy-willing states

We will talk to anyone; we will not arrange ourselves with regimes that are not at least democracy-willing.

Cooperation is acceptable only where it measurably reduces suffering and does not stabilise repression.

Trade is not a virtue. Dependency on unfree systems is strategic self-harm.

---

## 3) On peace and security

Peace is not the absence of conflict; it is the presence of reliable protections.

De-escalation without deterrence invites aggression; deterrence without diplomacy is blindness. We require both.

---

## 4) On truth and deception

Lies are infrastructure sabotage. Disinformation is an attack on society's nervous system.

We treat systematic deception as a security problem: transparency, traceability, audits, accountability.

---

## 5) On democracy

Democracy is not a ritual — it is a correction-capable system with built-in limits on power.

No one gets a veto over survival. If you block action, you must offer a workable alternative or yield the mandate.

---

## 6) On technology and AI

Technology amplifies intent. Without ethics it amplifies fear, greed, and control.

AI must be constitution-bound: rights-protecting, power-limiting, and responsibility-assignable.

---

## 7) On the inner condition

Fear is not a governing principle. Fear freezes life.

Dignity is not decoration; dignity is stability — and stability is the future.
