Here are “Schengen-/Euro-like” **coalitions of the willing** Europe’s democracies can do *without* waiting for unanimity—built as **intergovernmental treaties**, **EU-enhanced cooperation**, or **mixed clubs** that include democratic friends (UK, Norway, Canada, Japan, Australia, etc.).

## 1) European Defence Production & Procurement Club (EDPPC)

**Goal:** turn money into *real quantities* fast (ammo, drones, air defence, EW, radars).
**How:** a treaty that creates:

* a joint demand plan (multi-year),
* common specs (“one drone family”, “one 155mm standard set”),
* joint orders + guaranteed offtake,
* shared stockpiles and a wartime surge mechanism.
  **Who:** EU willing states + UK/Norway (industry) + Canada (industry/Arctic) as partners.

## 2) European Air & Missile Defence Shield (real, integrated)

**Goal:** integrated **air picture + layered defence** across Europe (drones → cruise → ballistic).
**How:** club with shared radar network, interceptor pooling, common training, and cross-border engagement rules (carefully defined).
**Who:** states that pay in and connect sensors/shooters; friends can join via tech + data links.

## 3) “Sanctions Enforcement Area” (SEA) – the anti-evasion club

**Goal:** make sanctions bite by killing loopholes.
**How:** treaty for:

* shared beneficial ownership registry access,
* joint enforcement teams,
* common penalties for evasion,
* coordinated export controls (dual-use),
* “no backdoor re-exports” compliance.
  **Who:** willing EU + UK + Norway + Switzerland (if they choose) + democratic G7 partners.

## 4) Energy Security & Resilience Pact (the “no blackmail” club)

**Goal:** end energy leverage (oil/gas, electricity, critical fuels).
**How:** joint strategic reserves, emergency swap lines, LNG/logistics pooling, grid interconnect priority, coordinated infrastructure protection.
**Who:** EU willing + UK/Norway (gas, offshore, grids) + Canada (LNG/critical minerals).

## 5) Critical Minerals & Industrial Sovereignty Club

**Goal:** secure supply chains (rare earths, nickel, lithium, graphite), processing, recycling.
**How:** common offtake contracts, shared strategic stockpiles, fast-track permits for refineries/recycling, coordinated investment screening.
**Who:** EU willing + Canada/Australia/Japan/Korea as natural partners.

## 6) “European Cyber Shield” (collective defence for networks)

**Goal:** rapid mutual help against cyber attacks and sabotage.
**How:** shared threat intel, joint hunt teams, minimum hardening standards for critical infrastructure, rapid attribution + coordinated response.
**Who:** EU willing + UK + Norway + democratic Indo-Pacific partners (intel/tech).

## 7) Counter-Disinformation & Influence Operations Pact

**Goal:** dry up foreign influence: money, media manipulation, political capture.
**How:** common transparency rules for political ads, funding, lobby contacts; joint investigations; coordinated platform requests; targeted sanctions.
**Who:** willing democracies—this scales well beyond Europe.

## 8) Rapid Border & Infrastructure Protection Force (hybrid threats)

**Goal:** protect pipelines, undersea cables, ports, rail hubs; assist borders under pressure.
**How:** standing multinational units + shared maritime/ISR assets + legal framework for deployment on request.
**Who:** Baltic/Nordic + Central Europe + Mediterranean willing; UK/Norway strong add-ons.

## 9) Ukraine Security Compact 2.0 (capability-first)

**Goal:** keep Ukraine superior (air defence, drones, ammo, ISR, training) with **automaticity**.
**How:** multi-year commitments, pooled procurement, “if X then Y” triggers (e.g., if major escalation → surge package).
**Who:** willing EU + UK + Canada + others.

## 10) Democratic Tech & Data Space (AI, compute, chips, cloud)

**Goal:** reduce dependency and secure sensitive workloads.
**How:** joint compute procurement, trusted cloud standards, export-control alignment, secure research collaboration.
**Who:** EU willing + UK + Canada + Japan/Korea.

# How to make these clubs “Schengen-/Euro-like” (i.e., durable)

A practical template:

1. **Clear entry criteria** (rule-of-law, security clearance standards, procurement transparency).
2. **Pay-to-play** budget + binding multi-year commitments.
3. **Governance that can act** (qualified majority *inside the club*, not unanimity).
4. **Mutual assistance clause** (scaled: intel → logistics → operational support).
5. **Open architecture**: EU members, EU partners, and selected democratic friends can join in tiers.
